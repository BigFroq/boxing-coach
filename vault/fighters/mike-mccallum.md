# Mike McCallum

## Summary
Mike McCallum, nicknamed "The Body Snatcher," is presented by Dr. Alex as one of the most technical fighters ever and the textbook model for body punching. According to the expert picks in the perfect boxer series, McCallum's surgical accuracy, consistent placement, and methodical approach to breaking opponents down over rounds make him the gold standard for bodywork, even if he didn't always score knockouts from it.

## What Alex Teaches
Dr. Alex highlights McCallum's body punching as the ideal technical framework for any fighter wanting to invest in the body. McCallum would bait opponents with his own jab, creating an opening, then slip and dig hooks into the body. This methodical process of breaking an opponent down round after round forces their guard to drop, which naturally creates openings upstairs. Alex emphasizes that McCallum's approach wasn't about one-punch knockout power to the body; it was the accumulation—the relentless investment paying dividends over time.

What set McCallum apart, in Alex's analysis, was his consistency and accuracy. He had the ability to place body shots between and around his opponent's guard, going around the sides with wide hooks to the midsection. His signature tools were the left hook to the liver and a sneaky right uppercut to the solar plexus. Alex teaches that McCallum didn't need to give angles; he could stand directly in front of an opponent and still find the route to the body, making him a perfect textbook fighter to study for body punching mechanics.

In the context of building a technically perfect boxer, body punching is a distinct fundamental skill area. Alex's expert panel placed McCallum as a superior body puncher over names like Julio Cesar Chavez and Canelo Alvarez because of his technical precision, even though Chavez turned body punching into the norm and Canelo received more public votes. McCallum's strategic body investment is framed as a core strategy for systematically dismantling opponents by attacking the foundation to collapse the structure.
