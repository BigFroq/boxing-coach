# Vasiliy Lomachenko

## Summary
Vasiliy Lomachenko is cited by Alex primarily as a benchmark for elite footwork and angle creation. Alex highlights how Lomachenko uses precise, efficient footwork to create and exploit angles, positioning himself beside or even behind opponents both offensively and defensively.

## What Alex Teaches
Alex points to Lomachenko as one of the top examples of footwork in boxing. In his ranking of the best boxers by area, Alex describes Lomachenko's footwork as "built around incredible angles." Lomachenko shuffles around opponents so quickly that he often ends up beside or even behind them, combining "ballet-like balance with boxing aggression" and never wasting steps.

Alex emphasizes that Lomachenko's technical and strategic use of footwork "is unreal and forms the foundation of his style." It isn't just that he can create angles — he is also exceptional at exploiting them perfectly, both to land shots and to escape his opponent's shots.

In the "Building the TECHNICALLY PERFECT Boxer" material, Lomachenko is chosen as the clear winner for best footwork. The angles he creates from his pivots work so well both aggressively and defensively, creating new angles to land shots and to evade. The sudden, unpredictable nature of his angles is described as reminiscent of Orlando Canizales, but with an overall game considered better. For the perfect composite boxer, Lomachenko's footwork was the selection.
