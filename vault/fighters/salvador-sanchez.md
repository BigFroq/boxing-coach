# Salvador Sanchez

## Summary
Salvador Sanchez was a masterful counter puncher known for exceptional anticipation and fluidity, punishing opponents who pressed too aggressively. His sharp punches, especially his right cross, were delivered with a precision that discouraged attackers and allowed him to counter effectively even in long, multi-punch sequences.

## What Alex Teaches
While Alex’s analysis of Salvador Sanchez is brief, he highlights qualities that align perfectly with his broader teachings on effective counter punching. Sanchez’s excellence lay in his ability to anticipate opponents’ moves, a skill Alex stresses as the foundation of counter punching—reading when an opponent is about to throw and pre-loading the cross-body kinetic chains to fire back instantly. The “sharp punches” Alex mentions, particularly that right cross, suggest Sanchez understood the four phases of a punch: he would drop his weight to load his tissues, then explode with hip rotation, stretch those cross-body chains to accelerate his arm, and follow through with weight transfer for a clean, impactful shot. His counters were so fluid and precise because he likely maintained a hip hinge and engaged lower back to stay balanced, allowing him to pivot and counter multiple punches without losing structure—much like the movement efficiency Alex sees in fighters like Benavídez. By discouraging opponents from pressing aggressively, Sanchez turned his defense into a weapon, embodying the concept of punishing every opening with tight, tension-free mechanics.
