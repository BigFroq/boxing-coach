# Julian Jackson

## Summary
Julian Jackson is the apex of one‑punch knockout power in boxing, with a staggering 89% KO ratio and equal devastation in both hands. Dr. Alex Wiant teaches that Jackson’s power is not just natural — it is mechanically perfect, built on a violent cascade of stretch‑shortening cycles through the cross‑body kinetic chains, executed with timing and “bad intentions” that leave opponents stiff.

## What Alex Teaches
Jackson personifies the mechanics Alex calls “technically perfect power.” In the “Perfect Boxer” build‑out, Jackson was selected for **Power** because his punching exploits the full sequence of load, explode, accelerate, and follow‑through with hip‑driven kinetic chain integration. Every shot begins with a lowering of the center of gravity to load elastic energy into the hips, then a violent push‑off that fires the hips into rotation **independently from the torso**. This independent hip rotation creates an immediate stretch in the cross‑body chains — primarily the spiral line and the front functional line — priming those chains to contract more violently. The torso is then forced to accelerate, and because the arm lags behind (continuing to move back or staying in horizontal abduction), even more stretch is developed across the shoulder joint in the pec and arm lines. The result is a whip‑like acceleration of the fist all the way to impact, just like a baseball pitcher uncorking a fastball.

Alex breaks down the sequence frame‑by‑frame in multiple knockouts. In the overhead view of the right hand against Francisco De Jesus, you can see Jackson’s belt line rotate while his upper body stays nearly still — “his hips are turning, creating stretch in those cross body chains… then the torso starts speeding up and his arm follows behind.” That stretch‑shortening cascade is repeated in every fight. Against Buster Drayton, the left hook shows a huge hip rotation relative to the torso, the arm lagging, then everything exploding together with a weight transfer that carries past the center line. Jackson’s straight right isn’t purely linear; Alex notes it has a **downward arc trajectory** that recruits more than just the pec, producing shearing force and delivering the punch with immense stability at impact.

Perhaps the ultimate demonstration is the Harold Graham knockout. Here Jackson’s arm is taken all the way back into **horizontal abduction**, like the bottom position of a dumbbell fly, creating maximum stretch on the pec and the shoulder‑crossing chains. At the very last moment before impact, his weight transfers from back to front while the arm suddenly accelerates relative to the already‑rotating torso. This hard‑stop position (the arm cannot go further back) eliminates any power leak by preventing shoulder absorption, ensuring every ounce of force goes into the target. Alex emphasizes that this is not an arm punch — it is a full‑body launching of the fist that illustrates the “kinetic cascade” of hips→torso→arm.

Intention is also central to Jackson’s power. Alex remarks that nearly all of his punches “have excellent mechanics… each one is meant to do damage,” and that “bad intentions” are part of maximizing punching power. Combine that constant offensive desire with brilliant timing, and you get a fighter who can land the knockout blow with either hand from almost any angle.
