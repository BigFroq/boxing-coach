# Rolly Romero

## Summary
In discussing Ryan Garcia’s fights, Dr. Alex points to Rolly Romero as a specific example of how a technical flaw—the stepping hook—was repeatedly exploited to put Ryan down. He uses this fight to illustrate how even slight mechanical errors can open a fighter up to clean, damaging counters.

## What Alex Teaches
Alex analyzes the Ryan Garcia vs. Rolly Romero fight within the broader context of Ryan’s recurring mistake: throwing a stepping hook that compromises balance. He explicitly notes that Ryan made the *same* error against Rolly that he did against Gervonta “Tank” Davis. The stepping hook—thrown while stepping forward—lined Ryan’s feet up and left him in a poor, upright position with shaky balance. As Ryan threw the hook, Rolly was able to counter with a punch that “pushed [Ryan] backwards even more off balance.” Alex highlights that Rolly didn’t just stop at the initial counter; he immediately followed up with a second hook that completed the knockdown. The teaching point is that when a fighter’s posture and momentum are already compromised by a technique that over-commits the weight, a sharp opponent—like Rolly—can capitalize with simple, well-timed counters that take full advantage of the off-balance state.

## Common Mistakes
- Throwing a stepping hook that over-commits the weight and lines up the feet, leaving the fighter off balance and vulnerable to counters—Ryan made this same error against both Tank Davis and Rolly Romero.
