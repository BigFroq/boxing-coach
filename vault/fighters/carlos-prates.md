# Carlos Prates

## Summary
Carlos Prates is an MMA striker who generates devastating knockout power through one of the sharpest striking patterns in the sport right now. His long arms and legs act as long levers, giving him a mechanical advantage, but the key is how he integrates his entire body into every punch with clean ground-to-glove sequencing through his kinetic chains. Alex teaches that Carlos breaks conventional rules—like keeping the head behind the knee—but does so strategically by maintaining his center of mass behind his base while fully accelerating through his target with structural stability at impact.

## What Alex Teaches
Carlos Prates exemplifies the four-phase punch system—load, explode, accelerate, follow through—executed with exceptional speed and precision. Alex highlights how Carlos loads his tissues by lowering his center of gravity, then immediately pushes off the ground upon foot contact. The load is so rapid that at 30 frames per second, some movement is lost between frames. As Alex explains, Carlos comes down, and the split second his foot touches the ground, he's already pushing off, extending his hip, extending his knee, and launching that hip forward into rotation. This hip extension uses the glutes to drive the entire kinetic sequence.

The critical component Alex emphasizes is how Carlos creates a lag in his arm relative to his rotating torso. As Carlos explodes into hip rotation, his elbow visibly lags back, stretching all the crossbody chains in his torso—specifically the arm lines and the front functional line. This quick stretch elicits a stretch shortening cycle in those muscles, the same phenomenon that powers a baseball pitch or tennis serve. Alex teaches that this stretch primes the muscles for a faster, more violent contraction, which launches the punch with devastating speed and power. The punch is thrown, not muscled or pushed—exactly the principle Alex teaches about how the shoulder transfers power rather than generates it.

Carlos also displays an aggressive body lean that Alex acknowledges breaks the traditional rule of keeping the head behind the knee. Alex teaches that while this is good basic advice, elite fighters like Carlos, Alex Pereira, and almost all favorite pros will extend themselves when they see an opening they want to capitalize on. The key is maintaining the center of mass behind the base. Carlos's center of mass remains controlled even as he leans forward aggressively, allowing him to transfer weight through the target while preserving enough stability to stay balanced. This is not reckless overcommitment—it's a calculated extension that maximizes reach and power transfer through the target.

Throughout the entire sequence, Carlos maintains full acceleration through impact with structural stability. Alex teaches that this clean ground-to-glove sequencing—from ground reaction force through the kinetic chains to the fist—is what separates natural punchers from those who merely arm-punch. The integration is seamless: load the tissues by lowering, explode with hip extension and rotation, allow the arm to lag and stretch the crossbody chains, then accelerate everything violently through the target. Alex points to Carlos as one of the best current examples in MMA of this complete, integrated system working at high speed.

## Common Mistakes
- Interpreting the "head behind knee" rule too rigidly and never extending for an opening, when elite fighters like Carlos strategically lean forward while keeping center of mass behind base to capitalize on opportunities
- Believing power comes from the shoulder or arm rather than understanding the punch is a throw powered by hip rotation and stretch shortening cycles through the kinetic chains
- Missing the importance of the lag—the arm must delay behind torso rotation to create the stretch that powers the acceleration, not move simultaneously with the body
