# Augie Sanchez

## Summary
Augie Sanchez serves as the opponent in Alex's case study of Prince Naseem Hamed's knockout power. The fight footage is used to demonstrate the loading, exploding, and follow-through phases of power punching, with Sanchez being on the receiving end of Hamed's compact yet devastating right hand and left hook.

## What Alex Teaches
Alex uses the Augie Sanchez fight to break down Prince Naseem's power generation mechanics frame by frame. The key teaching point is how Hamed loads his weight onto his rear foot, drops down, and then explodes through the target with hip rotation and arm acceleration. Alex emphasizes that this loading phase doesn't require a big, obvious wind-up — Hamed does it so fast that it's almost invisible unless you're watching for it specifically.

In the sequence where Hamed finishes Sanchez, Alex points out the short, compact nature of the punches. He stresses that it's not like bringing your fist all the way back, cocking it up, and then swinging around in a wild arc. Instead, it's about loading your rear foot or front foot (depending on the punch), dropping your weight down, falling forward, exploding with your hips, and punching through the target with follow-through.

Alex highlights one particular right hand where Hamed's fist travels about a foot past Augie's head, demonstrating the importance of follow-through in generating power. He also notes the moment where Hamed gives a little wink to his corner, which Alex interprets as a sign that Hamed knows he's starting to get on top and that Augie is in serious trouble.

The final knockout sequence is analyzed as a clean application of the boxing swing concept — the fall, the weight transfer, the moment of impact, and the extensive follow-through that puts Sanchez out. Alex uses this case study to validate his entire power punching blueprint, showing how Hamed's athleticism and leg strength translated into fight-ending power despite his unorthodox and sometimes fundamentally unsound style.
