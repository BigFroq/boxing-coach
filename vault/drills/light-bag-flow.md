# Light Bag Flow

## Summary
A low-intensity bag round used as warm-up or technical prep, where the boxer touches the bag rather than hitting it. The point is grooving the kinetic chain pattern — load, rotate, transfer, follow through — at a tempo where every link in the chain is felt and corrected. No power output is allowed; if a punch lands hard, you've defaulted to muscling instead of mechanics.

## What Alex Teaches

Light bag work is where you re-establish the foundation Alex emphasises throughout his teaching: relax until impact, push the ground rather than pivot, let the hips lead and the fist follow. At a light tempo every phase of the punch is observable. You can feel whether your weight is actually transferring forward past 50%, whether the rear foot is pushing or pivoting, whether the shoulder is stable or floating. At full tempo these errors are invisible because the body smooths them over with momentum.

Alex's pattern for a light bag round:
- **Round structure**: 2-3 minutes per round, 1 minute rest between. Treat it as warm-up, not output.
- **Pace**: every punch should be 40–60% of your normal output. If you start sweating through power, slow down.
- **Mental focus**: pick one mechanic per round (e.g., back-foot push on the cross, hip-led hooks, shoulder stability on the jab) and watch only that. Cycling through three mechanics across three rounds covers the basics.
- **Combinations**: keep them short — 2-3 punches max — so you can reset between and observe each link of the chain. Long combinations at light tempo lose mechanical discipline.

This is the kind of session you do daily, before any heavy bag work, to keep mechanics sharp without accumulating fatigue. Alex's principle: the bag tells you what your body is actually doing, but only if you're slow enough to listen.

## Common Mistakes

- **Drifting into medium intensity**: the most common failure. The bag is hypnotic; you start touching it and 90 seconds later you're swinging hard. Reset every minute by deliberately throwing slower than feels natural.
- **Long combinations**: 5+ punches at low tempo become rote and stop teaching anything. Keep it short, observe, reset.
- **No mental focus**: shadowboxing-on-the-bag without a specific mechanical target is just movement. Pick one phase of the punch each round.
