# Medicine Ball Plyometric Throw

## Summary
A partner-based plyometric power drill that trains the same kinetic chain reflex used to launch punches. Alex frames it as the *correct* way to use a medicine ball in a boxing gym — not as a static slam-and-stand exercise, but as a true plyometric movement where you catch on the fly and immediately throw back, taking the ball's incoming momentum as the load phase for your explosive return. This is heavy-output strength work performed in the gym setting; the goal is teaching the body to load and explode without thinking about it.

## What Alex Teaches

The wrong way Alex sees in most gyms: catch the ball, hold it, stand there, then turn around and throw. He says this gives "real limited results" because the catch and the throw are two separate movements — there's no plyometric stretch-shortening cycle, no real loading of the cross-body chains.

The right way is plyometric: catch on the fly and immediately throw back. The momentum of the incoming ball pushes you slightly backward — Alex calls this the load phase, identical to dropping back before a punch. From that loaded position you immediately explode forward and release. There is no pause between catching and throwing. The faster you can transition, the more your kinetic chains learn to load and fire reflexively.

Alex's structure for the drill: rotate roles with a partner. One person throws three times in a row while the other absorbs and returns plyometrically. Then switch. After both partners have run a set in their primary stance, switch to the weaker stance (southpaw if you're orthodox, orthodox if you're southpaw) and run another round. As the drill progresses, push the tempo — catch and explode faster each round. The ball coming back at you becomes the trigger for your next launch.

This is explicitly a *heavy* gym drill: it requires real medicine ball weight (10-20 lb depending on training age) and is meant to be exhausting after the prescribed sets. The point isn't volume — it's training the explosive kinetic chain pattern under load so the same pattern fires automatically when you punch.

## Common Mistakes

- **Catch-pause-throw**: the most common gym mistake. The pause kills the plyometric loading. If you can speak between the catch and the throw, you're doing it wrong.
- **Wrong weight**: too light = no real load on the chains; too heavy = the rebound is impossible to express explosively. Pick a weight where you can still throw it back at near-maximum speed.
- **Static stance**: feet should be loaded and active, not parked. The lower body absorbs and explodes; the arms are a relay.
- **Skipping the weak side**: Alex specifically prescribes both stances so the kinetic chain pattern is bilateral, not just dominant-side.
