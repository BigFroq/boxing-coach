# Rotator Cuff PVC Drill

## Summary
The Rotator Cuff PVC Drill is a pre-workout warm-up that activates the external rotators using a PVC pipe to create a brain-body connection. By alternating between pulling the bar apart and pushing it together while externally rotating, you recruit different stabilizing muscles in the shoulder without causing fatigue. This primes the rotator cuff to reduce movement and power leak at impact.

## What Alex Teaches
Alex teaches that when punching, the shoulder needs enough strength and stability in the rotator cuff to prevent a "power leak." If there is movement in the shoulder joint at impact, energy gets absorbed rather than transferred into the opponent. This drill is specifically a **warm-up** — not a strengthening exercise — designed to be done before your boxing workout so you don’t fatigue the delicate rotator cuff muscles before hitting the bag or sparring.

The exercise uses a PVC pipe. You set up with your elbow bent at 90 degrees and your shoulder abducted to 90 degrees from your body. From this position, you perform **external rotation** in two distinct ways:

1. **Pulling out**: Grip the bar and pull outward as if you’re trying to tear it apart. This recruits a lot of muscles in the posterior shoulder while you externally rotate.
2. **Pushing in**: Then switch to pushing the bar inward, as if trying to compress it toward the middle. This activates the muscles in the front of the shoulder and more specifically isolates the external rotators.

Perform 10–15 reps while pulling out, then 10–15 reps while pushing in. The goal is to establish a **mind-to-muscle connection** — when you have a better brain-body link, you can contract those muscles faster during your workout. Alex also shows a second movement for the subscapularis (internal rotator) using a band, moving the arm from high to low while pushing in and pulling out, but the core PVC drill focuses on external rotation.

He emphasizes not using any weight or resistance beyond the light PVC pipe for the warm-up. Strengthening the rotator cuff is done on separate off-days, never before a vigorous workout that involves pounding on the bag.

## Common Mistakes
- **Using weight or a resistance band for the warm-up** — the PVC pipe alone is plenty; adding load turns a neural activation drill into a strengthening exercise that will fatigue the cuff prematurely.
- **Doing too many reps or sets** — 10–15 repetitions per variation is enough to create the brain-body connection. Exceeding that before a workout risks wearing out the small stabilizers.
- **Skipping the isolate variations** — only pulling out or only pushing in misses the recruitment of different supporting muscles that help lock the shoulder at impact.
