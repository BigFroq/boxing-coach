Section 1: The 8 Style Dimensions

These are the axes of your fighter profile — derived from how Alex Wiant actually analyzes fighters. Each dimension is scored 0-100 based on your answers.

💥

Power Mechanics

How you generate force — hip-first sequencing, kinetic chain integration, throw vs push. Tyson/Wilder score high. Volume fighters score lower.

Maps to: Hip explosion, kinetic chains, shearing force, stretch-shortening cycle
🎯

Positional Readiness

Your ability to strike or respond at any moment. Weight distribution, pelvis neutrality, perpetual balance. Crawford is the gold standard.

Maps to: Stance discipline, weight distribution, ready-state maintenance
🧭

Range Control

Managing the "edge of the bubble" — staying at the boundary of striking distance, controlling when exchanges happen. Long-range technicians vs phone-booth fighters.

Maps to: Distance management, jab usage, footwork patterns, ring cutting
🛡️

Defensive Integration

How defense connects to offense — slipping into counters, rolling with shots, using head movement as a weapon. Toney seamlessly links defense to attack.

Maps to: Head movement, defensive transitions, counter-offense linking
🧠

Ring IQ & Adaptation

Reading opponents, adjusting tactics mid-fight, exploiting patterns. Crawford adapts constantly. Canelo repeats patterns regardless of what's working.

Maps to: Pattern recognition, tactical switching, opponent reading
⚡

Output & Pressure

Volume, pace, and forward pressure. How much output you generate and how relentlessly you apply it. Separates pressure fighters from sharp-shooters.

Maps to: Punch volume, combination length, forward movement, cardio capacity
🎭

Deception & Setup

Feints, attention manipulation, misdirection. Using "noise" punches to set up real ones. Floyd's psychological warfare, McClellan's jab-as-setup.

Maps to: Feinting, intention manipulation, psychological pressure
🔥

Killer Instinct

What you do when the opponent is hurt. Pour on pressure to finish, or stay patient and pick shots? Composure under the biggest moments vs aggressive finishing.

Maps to: Finishing ability, composure under pressure, aggression management

Why these 8? These map directly to how Alex analyzes fighters in his vault — hip mechanics, positional readiness, range ("edge of the bubble"), defensive transitions, ring IQ, output volume, attention manipulation, and finishing. Every question in the quiz feeds into one or more of these dimensions.
