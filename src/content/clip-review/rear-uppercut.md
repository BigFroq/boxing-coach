## What a good rep looks like

A hook rotated into the vertical plane, thrown with the rear hand. It follows the
same pattern as the straight: the **closing** of the hips, except instead of
shooting the arm forward the fighter shoots it vertically up. The elbow stays
close and the shoulder does nothing but transfer.

## Assess in this order

1. **Load onto the rear leg, and sink.** Weight loads onto the rear leg with the
   rear hip coiled back, pre-stretching the cross-body chains, and the body sinks
   to create the upward trajectory. Same start as the straight right.
2. **Hips close, driving forward and up.** The rear hip drives forward and upward
   at the same time while the front hip goes back. Along with the cross-body chains
   there is the lateral line powered by the oblique — look for that oblique stretch
   loading and then releasing.
3. **Elbow close, shoulder down.** The elbow stays close to the body at all times.
   **Shoulder down, arm up** — the shoulder stays neutral and only transfers power.
   Popping it disconnects the arm from the chains.
4. **Vertical accelerating arc.** Torso rotates around the spine, energy transfers
   through the chains into the arm, and the bicep mediates a fast acceleration as
   the arc radius tightens through impact.
5. **Impact and reset.** Grab the fist at impact — the peak of acceleration, with
   energy transferring up and slightly back. Rather than a forward lean, the body
   keeps moving in the punch's direction, down to up. Posterior shoulder muscles
   brake the arm and return it.

## If / then

- **If the elbow flares out** → force dissipates outward instead of vertically.
- **If the shoulder lifts or pops** → arm punch; the chain is severed. This is the
  headline fault on uppercuts.
- **If the fighter squats and pushes straight up** → linear mechanics, not a
  circular arc. Recommend the straight-arm swing drill, exaggerating hip rotation
  to feel chain tension, then shrinking the radius.
- **If the hips open instead of closing** → wrong hip action for a rear uppercut;
  it should mirror the straight right.
- **If the arm scoops with no hip drive underneath** → score Hip Explosion low and
  say the fist should feel driven up by the body.

## Do not flag

- **The body sinking before the punch.** That is the load.
- **Loading out of a slip, roll, or the end of a jab.** Legitimate entry into the
  load phase.
- **Landing with the palm facing the fighter.** Correct on uppercuts.
- **Continuing to move the body up and through** instead of leaning forward.
- **The fist stopping dead at contact on a heavy bag.**
