## What a good rep looks like

The overhand sits at one end of the straight → cross → overhand spectrum: a rear
hand that adds pronounced lateral torso bending on top of strict rotation, with a
bigger loop in the arm. Degree of lateral bend and degree of loop are what make it
an overhand rather than a cross — and distance decides how much is needed. It
lands with a downward angle, almost like a rear hook coming more downwards.

## Assess in this order

1. **Load.** The arm comes back as the body comes down. Weight drops, hips and
   torso coil, cross-body chains pre-stretch — the spiral line, the front
   functional line, the arm lines.
2. **Explode with side bend.** The hips push into rotation *with* lateral side
   bending. Turning away from the arm is what creates the stretch the chains then
   contract on. Check the fighter pushes off the rear foot rather than merely
   pivoting on it — pivoting rotates the hips but creates no torque, and without
   torque there is no acceleration.
3. **Acceleration relative to the hip.** The decisive check. The hip should nearly
   finish its rotation and *then* the arm accelerates relative to the pelvis into
   contact. If the fist arrives exactly as the hip reaches maximum forward
   position, there is no chain.
4. **Top-down arc, elbow bent.** The arc comes from up to down — that is what gets
   through a guard; down-to-up gets deflected or parried. A bend stays in the
   elbow; a fully straightened elbow runs out of steam at impact.
5. **Impact and lean.** Palm facing the fighter at impact — not turned over. The
   lean is aggressive, and the lead leg is what makes it safe: it stabilises the
   pelvis and stops the centre of mass travelling too far forward. Hinging at the
   hips is what keeps the core stable and leaves them able to get back out of range.

## If / then

- **If hip and hand move in lock step** → they are pushing the punch, not using the
  chains. This is the most common overhand fault. Score Energy Transfer low and say
  the arm must accelerate relative to the hip.
- **If the back foot pivots instead of pushing off** → no torque, no acceleration.
  Name the distinction; it is subtle on video.
- **If the elbow lifts and the shoulder leads** → a side delt raise. The connection
  between hips, torso and arm is lost and the arm becomes dead weight.
- **If the punch travels bottom-up** → easily parried. Re-shape the arc top-down.
- **If the fist turns over and lands on the back of the hand or knuckles** → cuts
  off the pec and the cross-body chains. Land palm facing you.
- **If the elbow locks out straight at contact** → the punch stops rather than
  driving body mass through the target.
- **If the lean has no lead-leg support underneath it** → balance and recovery are
  both gone; flag it even though the lean itself is correct.

## Do not flag

- **A pronounced lateral bend and a long looping arm path.** That is the punch. A
  bigger loop at longer range is correct, not sloppiness — distance is what dictates
  how much loop is needed.
- **An aggressive forward lean**, provided the lead leg is stabilising the pelvis.
- **Landing with the palm facing the fighter.** Correct.
- **The head moving well off the centre line.** Inherent to the punch.
- **The fist stopping dead at contact on a heavy bag.**
