## What a good rep looks like

A horizontal arc thrown with the rear hand, whose radius tightens through impact,
with the fist landing mid-arc to shear rather than perpendicular. Everything the
lead hook teaches about sequencing, the tightening arc, and impact timing applies.

The rear hook is driven by the hips **closing** — the rear side rotates forward,
the same action as the rear straight and rear uppercut — NOT the opening that
powers the lead hook. The load therefore sits on the rear leg, not laterally on
the lead leg.

## Assess in this order

1. **Load.** Weight settles onto the rear leg with knee and hip bend to explode
   from, cross-body chains pre-stretched, arm and shoulder briefly relaxed so the
   pec loads. The body should look alive, never stiff.
2. **Hip drive initiates; pivot follows.** The glute drives the rear hip forward
   and closed, starting with a push off the ground (flat foot, or a bounce off the
   ball of the foot) that extends the hip and knee to begin the rotation. As the
   body rotates the rear foot can then pivot — that pivot is fine, and helps keep
   the leg neutral with the pelvis. What it must NOT be is a pivot in place with no
   push behind it: that generates no torque.
3. **Hips – torso – arm.** The arm lags, the shoulder girdle retracts, the arm line
   stretches from pec to fist, then rebounds and snaps across the body with full
   shoulder-blade protraction. The hip must finish rotating while the arm is still
   travelling.
4. **Tightening arc.** Elbow flexes to shrink the radius and add speed at impact.
   Land mid-arc for shearing force.
5. **Impact and reset.** Grab the fist at impact as the weight crosses the centre
   line, keep rotating the body through contact rather than leaning forward, and
   counter-rotate the pelvis as the arm passes centre to kill over-rotation.

## If / then

- **If the hip and fist arrive together** → lockstep. Pushed, not thrown.
- **If the arm is hung far back to manufacture stretch** → the hips should be
  supplying it. Telegraphs badly.
- **If the shoulder pops before the impact window** → chain broken; protraction is
  the last movement of the acceleration phase.
- **If the fighter over-rotates with no counter-rotation** → balance is lost and
  the follow-up is gone.
- **If they finish needing the bag to hold them up** → recommend full-power reps in
  shadowboxing.

## Do not flag

- **The rear foot pivoting / turning after the hip-drive push.** On a closing
  punch that's correct, and helps keep the leg neutral with the pelvis. Only a
  pivot in place with no push behind it is a fault (see step 2).
- **The fist not turning over**, and **landing with the palm facing the fighter.**
  Both correct on hooks.
- **Continuing to rotate through impact** instead of leaning forward.
- **Loading out of a slip, roll, or the end of a previous punch.** Legitimate
  entry into the load phase.
- **The fist stopping dead at contact on a heavy bag.**
