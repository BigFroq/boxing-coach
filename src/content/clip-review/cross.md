## What a good rep looks like

The rear hand is driven by the **closing** of the hips. The cross specifically sits
mid-spectrum between the straight and the overhand: it adds lateral torso bending
on top of strict rotation, taking the head off the centre line and putting the
punch outside the opponent's jab. It lands with a slightly downward angle. Turning
*away* from the arm as it comes out is what creates the stretch the chains then
contract on.

## Assess in this order

1. **"On your mark."** Weight shifted back and loaded into the rear foot, tissues
   loaded, rear hip pulled back. Like a sprinter on the blocks. The power comes
   from shifting weight back then exploding the hips forward — the posterior chain
   runs through the calf, around the heel, into the foot, into the ground.
2. **Push, not pivot.** Phase 2 is a push: the rear hip drives forward to close.
   Like pushing a skateboard, except the foot stays planted and you push the ground
   back to turn the hip into rotation. A back foot that merely spins in place is
   the fault — it creates no torque.
3. **Hip–torso separation.** The hips rotate, the torso lags a beat behind. That
   lag is the torque. Then the torso rebounds and the arm lags, stretching the pec
   into a slingshot. Check across frames that the hip finishes its rotation while
   the arm is still travelling.
4. **The arc, top-down.** The arm accelerates on an arc, not a rail, coming from
   up to down — that is the path that gets through a guard. Bottom-up gets
   deflected or parried.
5. **Impact and follow through.** Grab the fist as hard as possible, pronate the
   forearm, drive arm and shoulder forward, transfer weight onto the lead leg with
   an aggressive forward lean. The arm is moving forward, rotating, and downward at
   once — that is the shearing force. The reset is elliptical; the arm does not
   come back the way it went out.

## If / then

- **If the hip and the fist arrive at the same moment** → lockstep. The punch is
  being pushed, not thrown. Score Energy Transfer low and say the hip must finish
  while the arm is still moving.
- **If the back foot pivots without pushing** → no torque, therefore no
  acceleration. Name the distinction explicitly; it is easy to miss on video.
- **If the elbow lifts and the shoulder leads** → this is a side delt raise, not a
  punch. The connection between hips, torso and arm is severed.
- **If the fighter throws bottom-up** → recommend re-shaping the arc top-down.
- **If the arm stops at the surface of the bag with no body rotation through it**
  → tension is being lost at impact; the punch dies on contact.
- **If the fighter is aiming well past the target** → the point of impact should
  be what they aim at, not two inches beyond it.

## Do not flag

- **Lateral side bend and the head coming off the centre line.** That is what
  makes it a cross rather than a straight. It is correct, not a balance fault.
- **Visibly relaxed, loose musculature before impact.** This is a benefit, not
  sloppiness — looseness until impact is the goal.
- **An aggressive forward lean at impact**, provided the lead leg is stabilising
  the pelvis. That lean is how weight transfers through the target.
- **The fist stopping dead at contact on a heavy bag.** Judge follow through by
  whether the body keeps rotating and whether the bag moves.
- **Finishing with weight on the lead leg and hips closed.** That is the correct
  end position and the natural start of a hook.
