# Straight Punch Mechanics

**Source:** Power Punching Blueprint by Alex Wiant DC (Pages 22-24)

## Straight Punch Breakdown

The straight (cross/rear hand) is the primary power punch, driven by closing the hips.

### The "On Your Mark" Position

Before the punch fires, the body is in the **"on your mark" position** — loaded, coiled, and ready to release. This is the fully loaded Phase 1 position where:

- Weight is shifted to the rear leg
- The rear hip is pulled back, pre-stretching the cross-body chains
- The body is coiled like a spring ready to unwind

### Phase 1: Loading

- Weight loads onto the **rear leg**
- Cross-body chains stretch from the rear hip to the lead shoulder
- The obliques are elongated, storing elastic energy

### Phase 2: Hip Closing

- The hips **close** — the rear hip drives forward powerfully
- The back foot pivots forward, initiating the rotation from the ground
- This is the most aggressive hip rotation of any punch — maximum torque generation

### Phase 3: Energy Transfer

- The **oblique stretch transfers into the serratus anterior** — a key chain link that connects the core rotation to the shoulder
- The serratus anterior protracts the scapula, extending the reach and channeling rotational energy into the arm
- The spiral line wraps the energy from the hip through the torso and out through the rear arm
- Each joint accelerates faster than the last: hip, core, shoulder, elbow, wrist, fist
- At impact: violent fist clench, forearm pronation, weight lands on the lead leg

### Phase 4: Follow Through

- The arm continues through the arc past the point of contact
- The body's forward momentum is absorbed and converted into the load for returning to stance
- The elastic rebound pulls the body back to neutral

## Key Detail: Oblique to Serratus Anterior Connection

The stretch through the **obliques** during hip rotation feeds directly into the **serratus anterior** muscle. This muscle wraps around the ribcage from the back to the front and is responsible for pushing the shoulder blade forward. This is the bridge that converts rotational core energy into linear arm extension — without strong serratus anterior engagement, hip power is lost before it reaches the fist.
