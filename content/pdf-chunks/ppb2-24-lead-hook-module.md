# PPB 2.0: Punch Module 3 — Lead Hook

**Source:** Power Punching Blueprint 2.0 by Alex Wiant DC (rough draft, Pages 41-44)

**Chains and muscles involved:**
- Phase 1-2: **lead leg lateral line and posterior chain**
- Phase 3-4: **spiral line, front functional line, arm line**

## Phase 1-2 — Load and Explode

Start with a slight forward weight bias, reached via:
- Loading through movement (normal weight shifting onto the front leg in stance)
- Beginning after your rear hand — hips closed, slight aggressive body lean
- Slipping off the jab to your lead leg

Your body should always feel **"alive," like a ready athlete** — never stiff or rigid. Relaxed fluidity lets muscles and connective tissues develop subtle elastic loading with every minor weight shift. **You are primed to launch the lead hook virtually any time you shift weight to your lead leg** with some knee/hip bend to explode from. Add an extra dip for a quick load when wanted. Let arm and shoulder relax briefly as you load the leg — the arm relaxes downward, **pre-stretching the upper pec fibers**.

**Drive with your glute from the hinged position off of a FLAT foot** to extend and rotate the hip — the glute does both functions, exploding the hips into rotation and forward drive, beginning to close the hips. **Pivoting on the lead foot won't use GRF as effectively as driving off a flat foot** — you have to drive off a flat foot to get the most out of phase 2.

The hips start turning to create independent hip/torso rotation, stretching the cross-body chains so they can rebound to begin body rotation — a relaxed body does this automatically. Not "loosy goosy" — an **elastic stretch and rebound, just like a sidearm throw**.

As the body begins rotating, simultaneously:
- The arm lags back
- The entire shoulder girdle **retracts** (moves all the way back)
- The elbow extends slightly

This stretches the **entire arm line from pec to fist**, ready to snap back in acceleration.

## Phase 3 — Accelerate

- As the torso reaches the end of its rotation, the pec reaches maximum stretch and begins to rebound
- Let the arm accelerate and **"snap" across the body** as you transfer weight past the center line
- The snap is driven by **full shoulder blade protraction** — shoulder blade and arm travel all the way forward
- **Flex the elbow by contracting the bicep** to maximize fist acceleration through the impact zone — flexing the elbow **reduces the diameter of the arc to increase speed at impact**
- The arc follows the **golden spiral**: begins broadly, rapidly tightens to full acceleration; shoulder snap + bicep contraction drive the final rapid acceleration
- Don't land perpendicular: **land mid-arc to create shearing force** — the arc is what lets you use the kinetic chains and the tether between fist and body

## Phase 4 — Impact and Follow Through

**Grab your fist at the moment of impact.** Your weight travels across the center line; as weight shifts to the rear leg, that shift can serve as the **load phase for a rear-hand follow-up**, or simply as loading to reset.

**The key to a hook reset is hip counter-rotation as your arm passes the center line** (practiced in the spaghetti arms drill): counter rotate the hips the opposite way — turn your belt buckle forward. This accomplishes:
- Upper-body momentum stretches the cross-body chains, **pre-loading them for a rear hand follow-up**
- Gets the hips back to neutral so you're ready to move

To reset: counter rotate hips, bring the arm back to the body, rotate the upper body back to stance.

**Third option**: roll into a defensive move — bob and weave, or slip.

## Hook Summary

- Shift weight to the lead leg to load it (+ optional extra fast load)
- Push off a **flat foot** using the glute to extend and rotate the hip
- Shoulder blade retracts, arm lags as the body turns, stretching the pec; elbow joint opens, bicep stretches
- As the torso reaches its end point the arm naturally rebounds and accelerates
- Arm accelerates and "snaps" across the body; arm and shoulder fully forward; bicep contracts to reduce arc radius and maximize fist speed
- Transfer weight at impact, counter rotate the hips, reset

## Key Takeaways

- **Drive off of a FLAT foot** to begin rotating the hips
- **Sequencing: hips – torso – arm**
- Body rotation makes the arm lag and the shoulder blade fully retract — when the body stops, the entire shoulder girdle **SNAPs** forward
- **Land with the arc** to generate shearing force at impact
- The arc trajectory closely follows the **golden spiral**: gradual start with the whole body, rapid acceleration through impact
