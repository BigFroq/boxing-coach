# PPB 2.0: The Front Functional Line

**Source:** Power Punching Blueprint 2.0 by Alex Wiant DC (rough draft, Pages 12-13)

The front functional line is a kinetic chain that connects the legs, pelvis and torso to the arm through the **adductors, abs and pec major**. It plays a major role in arm acceleration, forward force transfer and stability at impact.

This chain connects the lower body to the upper body during punching. As the body accelerates forward, these structures help transfer force into the arm while stabilizing the shoulder and torso at impact.

## Main Functions in Punching

- Connects the torso and pelvis to the arm
- Assists with arm acceleration and forward force transfer
- Helps stabilize the torso, shoulder and arm at impact
- Assists with shoulder protraction and weight transfer
- Helps link rotational force into arm trajectory and acceleration

## Key Structures

- **Rectus abdominus (abs)** — stabilizes the torso during punching and connects upper body to lower body through the anterior core. As the body accelerates forward, the abs maintain stability and assist with effective weight transfer at impact.
- **Pec major** — connects the torso to the arm and plays a major role in the **acceleration phase**. Drives shoulder horizontal adduction and forward arm acceleration while stabilizing the arm at impact.
- **Latissimus dorsi (context structure)** — primarily part of the posterior/back functional line, but included here because it works closely with the front functional line during punching: provides **downward arm trajectory** and contributes to shoulder stability and force transfer at impact.
