# Hand Wrapping Guide

**Source:** Power Punching Blueprint by Alex Wiant DC (Pages 28-35)

## Wrap Type

Use **Mexican style stretchy wraps**. These conform to the hand better than traditional cotton wraps and provide both support and compression.

## 15-Step Hand Wrapping Method

### Step 1: Loop on Thumb
Place the thumb loop over your thumb with the wrap coming off the back of the hand.

### Step 2: Around the Wrist
Wrap around the wrist 2-3 times to create a stable base. Keep it snug but not too tight.

### Step 3: Across the Back of the Hand
From the wrist, bring the wrap diagonally across the back of the hand toward the knuckles.

### Step 4: First Knuckle Wrap
Wrap across the knuckles once to begin building the knuckle pad.

### Step 5: Back to Wrist
Return to the wrist to anchor the knuckle wrap.

### Step 6: Second Knuckle Wrap
Come back up and wrap across the knuckles a second time.

### Step 7: Back to Wrist Again
Return to the wrist to anchor.

### Step 8: Third Knuckle Wrap
Wrap across the knuckles a third time. You now have **6 total layers** of padding across the knuckles (3 passes, each creating 2 layers as the wrap folds over itself).

### Step 9: Finger Weaving - Between Pinky and Ring Finger
From the knuckles, bring the wrap between the pinky and ring finger, around the finger, and back across the back of the hand. This locks the knuckle pad in place.

### Step 10: Finger Weaving - Between Ring and Middle Finger
Repeat: bring the wrap between the ring and middle finger, around and back across the back of the hand.

### Step 11: Finger Weaving - Between Middle and Index Finger
Repeat: bring the wrap between the middle and index finger, around and back across the back of the hand.

### Step 12: Lock the Finger Weaving
Wrap around the wrist once to anchor all the finger weaves. The weaving locks the knuckle pad so it cannot shift during impact.

### Step 13: Across the Palm
Wrap across the palm once, but keep the **palms largely uncovered** — you need grip flexibility.

### Step 14: Grab Wrap Under Knuckles
**Grab the wrap under the knuckles** — this creates a fist-shaped pad that sits in the palm when you make a fist, filling the gap and providing internal support.

### Step 15: Finish at Wrist
Wrap remaining material around the wrist and secure with the velcro closure.

## Key Principles

- **6 total layers** across the knuckles for maximum protection
- **Finger weaving** locks the knuckle pad in place so it does not shift on impact
- **Palms stay uncovered** — you need to be able to grip and grab the wrap under the knuckles
- The wrap should feel snug when you make a fist but not cut off circulation when the hand is open
- The knuckle pad and finger weaving work together to create a protective structure that moves with your hand
