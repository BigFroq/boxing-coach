# What is a Power Punch?

**Source:** Power Punching Blueprint by Alex Wiant DC (Pages 1-2)

## Power = Mass x Velocity

Newton's second law says that Force = Mass x Acceleration. In a punch, you need to get your fist moving as fast as you can, so you can deliver a maximum force into your opponent.

## The Goal

The goal of a power punch is to stop your opponent.

When targeting the head, the desired outcome is a concussion of the brain. A concussion occurs when the brain is rapidly accelerated. When this rapid acceleration occurs, the cerebrospinal fluid that surrounds the brain can't adequately cushion the brain's rapid movement, and the brain hits the inside of the skull. This is what occurs in a knock down or knock out.

A large force to the body can hurt quite a bit and stop an opponent as well, however that is typically due to pain or the inability to breathe, rather than a loss of consciousness.

## Three Types of Acceleration

- **Rotational acceleration**: occurs when the head spins on its axis (the spine). A punch to the tip of the jaw creates this.
- **Linear acceleration**: can occur from a straight punch to the face or jaw.
- **Angular acceleration**: can occur from a punch to the temple or side of the head.

## Key Principle: Throw, Don't Push

You CAN'T generate the type of acceleration and velocity necessary if you look at a punch as a planar movement, or a straight line motion. In sports where it's important to transfer high speed energy into another object (baseball, golf ball, tennis ball), the mechanics include rotational acceleration, or torque.

In a planar movement (bench press, squat), peak energy production occurs at the beginning. In a throw, swing or serve, the peak acceleration comes in the middle to end of the movement.

**To generate real power in a punch you have to think of it as having similar mechanics to a throw or swing, and have the peak power generation towards the end of the movement, rather than the beginning.**
