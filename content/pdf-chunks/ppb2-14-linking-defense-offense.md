# PPB 2.0: Linking Defense and Offense — Loading Through Movement

**Source:** Power Punching Blueprint 2.0 by Alex Wiant DC (rough draft, Pages 19-20)

You can utilize your bamboo-like spinal flexibility in defensive movements — **slips, bobs and weaves**. These movements can load the legs and core just like the loading phase of a punch.

## Why Defensive Loading Beats Normal Loading

When you link defense and offense this way you can create **even more loading than you would in a normal punch**, because the defensive movement uses a much larger range of motion. The larger range of motion gives more opportunity to "load up" elastic muscle energy than a short, compact punch could.

**You are hiding significant loading within the defensive move itself.** This is the concept of **"loading through movement,"** which can add a tremendous amount of power to your punch.

## The Slip Example

- When you slip with the hinge, move your center of mass over the leg you're slipping toward: slip left → weight shifts over the left leg; slip right → weight over the right leg. That side of the body is being loaded
- As you reach the end of the slip, the body may bend slightly in that direction **like bamboo** — the core muscles stretch during this process
- This means your **entire body** — legs AND core — is being loaded
- You can then use this loading to **seamlessly snap off a power punch from that position**: instead of being strictly defensive, the slip becomes a ballistic movement that explodes into a powerful punch

(Related applications elsewhere in the manual: the slip counter loads the rear leg for the rear hand; the pull counter shifts weight to the back leg as its load phase; slipping off the jab onto the lead leg is one entry into the lead hook.)
