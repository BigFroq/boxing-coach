# PPB 2.0: The Lateral Line and Quads — The Jab Engine

**Source:** Power Punching Blueprint 2.0 by Alex Wiant DC (rough draft, Pages 14-15)

The lateral line and quads create **back-to-front movement and backwards hip rotation to power the jab**.

The lateral line uses ground reaction force (GRF) to move the body laterally. **In a bladed stance, this lateral movement translates into forward movement.** The quads help create hip opening and rotational loading for the jab. These muscles work together with the spiral, front functional and arm lines to transfer force from the ground through the torso into the fist.

## Main Functions in Punching

- Use ground reaction force to rotate your rear hip posteriorly and move laterally (forward in a bladed stance) when throwing a jab
- Open the hips by rotating the rear hip posteriorly
- Stabilize the lead hip in lead hooks and uppercuts
- Provide stability to the lead leg in rear hand punches
- Transfer force into the cross-body kinetic chains through hip rotation and forward/lateral movement

## Key Structures

- **Lateral line** — begins at the side of the foot, travels up the outside of the leg into the **glute medius, glute minimus and TFL**. Creates lateral movement using ground reaction force; in a bladed stance this lateral force translates to forward movement for the jab.
- **Quadriceps** — extend the knee and push the foot off the ground to create **posterior hip rotation in the rear hip**. Contribute heavily to posterior hip rotation and hip opening during the jab, and create the back-to-front body movement that drives the jab forward. Also provide leg/pelvis stability in rear-hand shots (rear uppercut, rear hook, straight, cross, overhand).

## Integration with Other Lines

The lateral line and quads feed force into the spiral line, front functional line and arm lines, which then transfer that force into torso rotation, arm acceleration and impact stability.
