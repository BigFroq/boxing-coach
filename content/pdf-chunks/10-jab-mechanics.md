# Jab Mechanics

**Source:** Power Punching Blueprint by Alex Wiant DC (Page 22)

## Jab Breakdown by Phase

### Phase 1: Loading

- Weight shifts to the **rear leg**, loading elastic potential energy
- The lead side of the body is pre-stretched through the cross-body chains
- Sink slightly to engage the ground connection

### Phase 2: Hip Opening

- The hips **open** (lead hip pulls back slightly, then drives forward)
- The back foot pivots to initiate rotation
- Torque is generated through rapid rotational acceleration of the hips

### Phase 3: Energy Transfer

- Energy transfers from the hips through the **core** and into the lead shoulder and arm
- The cross-body chains (functional lines) that were stretched now contract rapidly
- The **lateral line** stabilizes the torso during the transfer
- The arm extends along its arc, with the fist accelerating through each successive joint
- At impact: fist clenches violently, forearm snaps with pronation, weight transfers forward

### Phase 4: Follow Through

- The arm continues past the point of impact along the natural arc
- Forward weight transfer is used as the load to spring back to neutral
- The body resets to fighting position through the elastic rebound

## Key Chains Used

- **Superficial front line** and **superficial back line** — loading and release
- **Back and front functional lines** — cross-body power transfer
- **Lateral line** — stabilization during rotation
- **Arm lines** — final delivery of force through the arm to the fist
