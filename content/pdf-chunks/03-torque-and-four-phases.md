# Torque Generation and the Four Mechanical Phases of a Punch

**Source:** Power Punching Blueprint by Alex Wiant DC (Pages 7-8)

## How Athletes Generate Torque

Other rotational athletes — baseball players, golfers, tennis players — all generate power through the same fundamental mechanism: **torque**. The body creates rotational force by sequentially accelerating segments from the ground up, each segment building on the momentum of the one before it.

## The Four Mechanical Phases

### Phase 1: Loading Elastic Potential Energy

- **Weight transfer and gravity** pull the body into a loaded position
- Muscles are **elongated** (stretched) to store elastic potential energy
- Think of pulling back a rubber band — the further you stretch, the more energy is stored
- This loading happens through a combination of gravity-assisted weight shift and deliberate muscle lengthening

### Phase 2: Release Into Hips to Create Torque

- The stored energy is **released into the hips** through rapid rotational acceleration
- The hips act as the primary torque generator
- This rotational energy is then **transferred into the core**
- The key is the speed of rotation — rapid acceleration creates significantly more force than slow turning

### Phase 3: Transfer of Energy Through Core Into Shoulder and Arm

- Energy flows from the core into the **shoulder, then the arm**
- Each successive joint in the chain **accelerates faster** than the previous one
- By the time energy reaches the fist, it has been amplified through every link in the chain
- This is why a punch's power comes from the whole body, not just the arm — the fist is simply the final delivery point

### Phase 4: Follow Through

- **Continuation past the point of energy release**
- Includes the natural **rotation and unwinding** of the body after impact
- Follow through is not wasted motion — it ensures maximum energy transfer into the target
- Prevents injury by allowing the body to decelerate gradually rather than stopping abruptly
