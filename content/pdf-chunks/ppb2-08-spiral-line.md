# PPB 2.0: The Spiral Line

**Source:** Power Punching Blueprint 2.0 by Alex Wiant DC (rough draft, Pages 11-12)

The spiral line is a continuous **myofascial kinetic chain** that connects the lower body to the upper body in a spiral pattern. It plays a major role in rotation, rotational acceleration, force transfer and stability during punching.

Rather than producing force in a purely linear direction, the spiral line helps create **rotational tension across the body**. As the hips and torso rotate, these structures stretch, are loaded with elastic muscle energy, then recoil to accelerate the torso and arm — and then stabilize the arm at impact.

## Main Functions in Punching

- Creates rotational force and torso acceleration
- Transfers force diagonally across the body
- Helps connect hip rotation to upper body rotation
- Assists with shoulder protraction and arm acceleration
- Creates stability and tension at impact for an effective weight transfer

## Key Structures

- **Internal oblique** — runs diagonally upward toward the center of the abdomen, connects into the linea alba. As the hips rotate it stretches and contracts to help rotate the torso and create cross-body tension for stability and force transfer. **Acts on the side of the body between the torso and the hip that is rotating backwards.**
- **External oblique** — runs diagonally from the ribs toward the abdomen, connects into the internal oblique through fascial connections at the linea alba. Helps rotate the torso around the body's center of rotation and contributes to rotational acceleration. **Acts on the side of the body between the torso and the hip rotating forwards.**
- **Serratus anterior** — connects with the external oblique and links the spiral line to the shoulder blade. **Protracts the shoulder blade (pushes the shoulder forward) as the last part of the acceleration phase** and helps stabilize the arm during impact and follow-through. Effectively links the core to the arm during rotational punching movements.
