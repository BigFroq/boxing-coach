# Bag Work, Breathing, and Combinations

**Source:** Power Punching Blueprint by Alex Wiant DC (Pages 36-39)

## Equipment

- Use **16oz well-padded gloves** for bag work — protects the hands and builds endurance
- A **water bag** is recommended over sand or traditional filled bags
  - Recommended brands: **Powair**, **Title**
  - Water bags absorb impact more naturally and are easier on the joints

## Weight Control Exercise

This exercise develops the feel of transferring body weight into the bag.

1. Stand in front of the bag in your fighting stance
2. **Push your fist into the bag with your entire body weight** — lean into it, let the bag support you
3. Feel what it is like to have your full weight behind the fist
4. Then let the bag push back — **catch the returning bag** as it swings toward you
5. Absorb its weight and redirect it

This teaches the sensation of true weight transfer at impact rather than just arm punching.

## Breathing

**It does not matter where you are in the breathing cycle when you punch.** You always have enough air available to create **intra-abdominal pressure** (the core bracing that stabilizes your torso during impact).

- You do not need to exhale sharply on every punch
- You do not need to time your punches to your breath
- At any point in the breath cycle — inhaling, exhaling, holding — there is sufficient air in the lungs to brace the core

### Breathing Exercise

To prove this to yourself:

1. Hit the bag at **different stages of your breath cycle**:
   - While inhaling
   - While exhaling
   - At the top of a full inhale
   - At the bottom of a full exhale
   - While holding your breath
2. Notice that you can generate solid power at every stage
3. This eliminates the anxiety of timing breath to punches and allows you to punch freely

## Combination System

Combinations flow naturally from the hip mechanics of each punch. The key principle: **each punch's follow-through naturally loads the next punch.**

### The Flow Logic

- **Jab** opens the hips — the reset (closing the hips) naturally loads the **straight**
- **Straight** closes the hips — the reset (opening the hips) naturally loads the **hook**
- Each punch creates the loading position for the next

### Listed Combinations

1. **Jab - Straight** (1-2)
   - Jab opens hips, reset closes hips into straight

2. **Jab - Jab - Straight** (1-1-2)
   - Double jab with hip opening, then close into straight

3. **Jab - Straight - Hook** (1-2-3)
   - Jab opens, straight closes, reset opens into hook

4. **Jab - Straight - Hook - Straight - Jab** (1-2-3-2-1)
   - Full combination flowing through alternating hip open and close cycles
   - Each punch's follow-through loads the next naturally

### Key Principle

Do not think of combinations as separate punches strung together. Think of them as **one continuous chain of hip rotations** where each opening and closing naturally produces the next punch in the sequence.
