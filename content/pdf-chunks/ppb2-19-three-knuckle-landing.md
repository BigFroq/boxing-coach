# PPB 2.0: Module 5 — What Knuckles to Strike With? (Three-Knuckle Landing)

**Source:** Power Punching Blueprint 2.0 by Alex Wiant DC (rough draft, Pages 30-31)

In the KIMs framework, **you should land with your last three knuckles** rather than the first two (with wraps and boxing gloves — bare knuckle or MMA may have different considerations).

## The Lat Connection

You may have heard "lead with the pinky" when doing rows to stimulate the lats. Alex doesn't want you leading with the pinky, but: **your last three fingers all have connections down the inside part of the arm with your lats.** You want to utilize this connection for stability at impact in all your punches.

## The Wrist Flexor Connection

You also want to use the strong wrist flexors (front of the lower arm) for impact stability. Punches travel in an arc; when you land with this arc and **grab your fist at impact**, the wrist flexors stabilize the wrist while maintaining continuous tension in the kinetic chains. Using the wrist flexors for stability lets you use more of these muscles when landing with the last three knuckles.

## Why Two-Knuckle Landing Became Popular — and Why Alex Disagrees

- The two-knuckle norm comes from fear of the **"boxer's fracture"** (fracture of the ring-finger metacarpal)
- But it's *always* possible to break your hand — just as it's possible to break your nose, jaw or orbital bone. "You can die in the ring. There are risks."
- Even intending a two-knuckle landing, you can land wrong accidentally
- With wraps and gloves, intentionally landing with three knuckles **while utilizing shearing force at impact**, your connective tissues and muscles absorb more of the impact rather than transferring it directly to the bones (hand breaks remain possible)

"You are free to do what you think is most reasonable. However, even if you think I'm wrong — try it. I think you may prefer the three-knuckle landing."
