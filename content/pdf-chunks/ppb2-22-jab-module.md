# PPB 2.0: Punch Module 1 — Jab (and Jab to the Body)

**Source:** Power Punching Blueprint 2.0 by Alex Wiant DC (rough draft, Pages 36-38)

**Chains and muscles involved:**
- Phase 1-2: primarily the **lateral line and quads** (posterior chain may contribute glute-mediated hip extension depending on position)
- Phase 3-4: **spiral line, front functional line, arm lines**

## Phase 1-2 — Load and Explode

Start ~50/50, legs slightly loaded from the athletic hinged stance. Two main ways to load:

- **Natural loading** from movement or weight shifting — loading through movement helps **disguise your intention** while staying ready to strike. Use back-to-front-to-back weight transfer to shift weight to the rear leg
- **Dynamic loading** — quickly relax the rear leg to bend at hip and knee; weight drops slightly to the rear leg, then bounce out of it with phase 2

To begin phase 2: **kick off your back foot using your quads and lateral leg muscles.** The push-off comes from the bottom and front-outside edge of the foot to utilize the lateral leg muscles. Goal: **flick your rear hip backwards** and create lateral movement forward (forward in a bladed stance = toward the target).

### Stepping When You Punch

Stepping with the jab closes distance for more reach, uses gravity to move forward, and lets you catch your weight with the phase-4 weight transfer. When you load the rear leg with the quick dip, **simultaneously lift the lead leg** to get ready for the step. You can also stretch the lead arm's cross-body chains during the dip by briefly relaxing the lead arm and shoulder.

## Phase 3 — Accelerate

The hip flick backwards/sideways has stretched the cross-body chains; utilize the stretch with an elastic rebound to rotate the body — weight begins moving forward. As the body rotates and moves forward, **the arm launches forward in sequence**. When the body finishes its rotation, the arm should reach full acceleration crossing into the impact zone. **The body rotation isn't huge with this punch — subtle but important for launching the arm.**

## Phase 4 — Impact and Follow Through

Simultaneously:
- Transfer the majority of weight to the lead leg (keep closer to 50/50 if following up with the rear hand)
- Drive arm and shoulder forward all the way
- Grab your fist hard
- Continue accelerating through the target

The arm should travel on a **slight downward trajectory at the moment of impact** to leverage shearing force for stability. Weight behind the rigid arm moves through the target with the desired amplitude. Then: let the arm unwind, bring it back, push off the lead leg to get back into position — or use the jab's end point to begin the rear hand (keep weight closer to 50/50; if you stepped far, get your base back before throwing the 2).

## Jab Summary

- Load the rear leg with a quick dip as you lift the lead leg and momentarily relax the lead arm
- Kick off the rear foot, sideways and forwards, to flick the rear hip backwards
- The rear hip rotation stretches the cross-body chains which rebound to accelerate
- The torso's rotation and forward movement **slingshot the arm forward**; keep the arm moving in an arc to maintain tension
- At impact grab your fist, snap the arm forward, land with your bodyweight as you step to catch forward momentum

## Jab to the Body

Quite similar to the traditional jab, with a **more horizontal body position created by an increased hip hinge**. More cross-body chain recruitment at impact because the arm travels a steeper downward trajectory.

- When catching forward momentum with the lead leg, let knee and hip bend slightly to absorb the weight — this loads the lateral line and lead-leg glute so you can bounce back to the start
- **The basketball jab-step analogy**: like a player stepping sideways to shake a defender, then rebounding off that foot. The lead leg catches your momentum, loads like a spring, then rebounds to push you back into your stance
- With KIMs mechanics you can **start out of range, extend with your body to land, then reset back out of range**

## Key Takeaways

- Launch from a quick dip — from stance, or added to a backward weight transfer for a faster, snappier jab
- Disguise your loading with natural movement when possible
- **The hip flick drives phase 3**
- **The arc maintains tension in the lead arm**
