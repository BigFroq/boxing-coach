# PPB 2.0: Module 2 — Phase 1: Load (Natural vs Dynamic Loading + Bounce Exercises)

**Source:** Power Punching Blueprint 2.0 by Alex Wiant DC (rough draft, Pages 24-26)

## Natural vs Dynamic Loading

Two types of loading used throughout the KIMs system:

- **Natural loading** — created automatically by your movement. Whenever your weight shifts onto a leg, the muscles and connective tissues briefly develop elastic energy as they accept your body weight. If you launch immediately from that weight shift ("touch and go"), you're using natural loading.
- **Dynamic loading** — an additional, **intentional** increase in elastic loading. If you've become stationary on a leg and the initial elastic energy has faded, add a **quick dip** to recreate it: briefly relax the leg to allow a rapid bend at the hip and knee, then immediately rebound into the punch. Add dynamic loading any time you want extra speed and power.

## The Load Phase Concept

Load phase = rapidly lowering your athletic center and loading your muscles. In stance your legs are already somewhat loaded; extra dynamic loading gives more speed and power on demand. It's the same quick drop before sprinting forward, or the dip before a jump.

- Very fast — only a dip of **around one inch**
- Mediated by a very fast **relaxation** of the legs to slightly increase knee/hip bend
- Keep the same neutral core, spine and pelvis from the hinge — **the dip comes strictly from the legs**
- Should feel like the imaginary underground weight pulls you down at "freefall" speed using gravity
- Primarily uses the posterior chain and lateral line (plus quads for the jab)

## Exercise: A Bounce in Your Step (4 Versions)

No fixed reps — the more you do it the more natural it feels. Keep spine, core and pelvis neutral; bend only at hip and knee.

- **Version 1**: In stance, practice dropping about an inch and bouncing up. No jump — drop, catch, rebound to your original stance.
- **Version 2**: From 50:50, shift weight back slightly (~40:60), hold a second, then dip on the back leg with a quick relaxation and bounce your weight forward to ~60:40 front. Return to 50:50 and repeat on the front leg.
- **Version 3**: Bounce weight continuously between legs (no return to neutral). Relax arms and shoulders so they drop slightly while loading the legs — the leg dip lets the arm and shoulder muscles stretch simultaneously. This prepares you for simultaneous cross-body chain + leg loading later. Rhythm: 50:50 → quick rear-leg dip to 40:60 → bounce off, move athletic center forward, load lead leg 60:40 → bounce back. Feel the lateral leg muscles load and unload (the lateral line moves you forward in stance).
- **Version 4 — The Bounce Step**: Feet on the same line about shoulder width. Step back, dip as the rear leg contacts the ground to load it, then bounce off the rear leg to launch your athletic center forward to the starting position. Both legs.

## Big Takeaway

Whenever your weight shifts onto a leg, that leg is naturally being loaded. **A smooth weight transfer will usually create enough elastic loading to launch a punch on its own.** If you've gone stationary and the loading has faded, a quick dip reloads the leg before exploding into the punch.
