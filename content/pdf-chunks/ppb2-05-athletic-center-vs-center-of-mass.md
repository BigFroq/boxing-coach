# PPB 2.0: Center of Mass vs Athletic Center

**Source:** Power Punching Blueprint 2.0 by Alex Wiant DC (rough draft, Pages 7-8)

Two concepts that sound similar but are quite different:

## Center of Mass (COM)

Your center of mass is a **dynamic point** — it changes based on your body's position. It's the center balance point of your body, changing with posture, limb position, body lean, etc. You need to be conscious of it for **balance, stability, and using ground reaction force optimally**.

Example: if you stand on one leg, your center of mass has to be over that leg to maintain balance.

## Athletic Center

Your athletic center is different. In martial arts there is a term, "center" — a point about **two inches below your belly button in your pelvis**. Traditional names: **hara** (Japanese), **dantian** / "lower dantian" (Chinese). Alex calls this point the **athletic center**.

- It is the **"hub" your body organizes rotational force around** during punching and other athletic movements
- When your legs turn your hips and pelvis, they turn around your athletic center
- This rotation is what stretches your cross-body kinetic chains and allows them to snap back to drive the rest of the body's kinetic cascade

## Coaching Cue

When performing the movements in the manual, **focus on "leading" the movements with your athletic center. It is the "source" of your movement and power.** (This cue recurs throughout the phase modules: imagine a huge weight attached to your athletic center pulling you down; lead hip rotation with the athletic center to create hip/torso separation.)
