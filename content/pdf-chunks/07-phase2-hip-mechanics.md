# Phase 2: Hip Opening and Closing Mechanics

**Source:** Power Punching Blueprint by Alex Wiant DC (Pages 16-17)

## Two Types of Hip Movement

The hips generate torque through two distinct movements: **opening** and **closing**. Different punches are powered by different hip actions.

### Opening the Hips

The hips rotate to open up the body (turning the lead hip back). This movement powers:

- **Jab**
- **Hook**
- **Uppercut** (lead hand)

### Closing the Hips

The hips rotate to close the body (driving the rear hip forward). This movement powers:

- **Straight punches** (cross/right hand)
- **Rear hand uppercut**

## Phase 2 Exercise: Hip Rotation Drill

**Volume: 100 reps per day, both orthodox and southpaw stances.**

### How to perform:

1. Stand in your fighting stance
2. **Pivot on the back foot** to initiate the rotation
3. **Push forward** to close the hips — the rear hip drives toward the front, rotating the torso
4. **Push backward** to open the hips — the lead hip pulls back, opening up the body
5. Focus on generating the rotation from the ground up through the foot pivot
6. Keep the upper body relaxed and let the hip rotation naturally move the torso

### Key points:

- Train **both stances** (orthodox and southpaw) to develop both sides equally
- The pivot on the back foot is the engine — this is where the rotation initiates
- Forward pivot = closing hips = straight punch power
- Backward pivot = opening hips = jab, hook, uppercut power
- Build up to 100 reps per side daily for consistent development of rotational power
