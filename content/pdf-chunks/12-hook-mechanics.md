# Hook Mechanics

**Source:** Power Punching Blueprint by Alex Wiant DC (Pages 24-25)

## Hook Breakdown

The hook is powered by hip opening and uses a lateral weight load to generate devastating rotational force.

### Phase 1: Lateral Weight Load

- Weight loads to the **lead leg side** (lateral loading)
- The body shifts laterally, stretching the lateral line and cross-body chains on the lead side
- This lateral load is distinct from the front-to-back loading of the jab and straight

### Phase 2: Hip Opening

- The hips **open** — the lead hip drives the rotation
- The torque generated is horizontal, matching the horizontal trajectory of the hook
- The pivot comes from the lead foot, rotating the body around the spine axis

### Phase 3: Energy Transfer and Arc

- Energy transfers from the hips through the core into the lead shoulder
- The **bicep reduces the arc radius** — by bending the elbow, the fist travels a tighter arc, increasing speed at impact (same principle as pulling arms in during a spin)
- The arm maintains approximately a 90-degree angle at the elbow
- The tighter radius means the fist accelerates faster through the point of contact

### Phase 4: Follow Through

- **Weight passes the midline** — the body's center of gravity moves past the centerline during follow through
- Posterior shoulder muscles **brake** the arm after impact to prevent over-rotation
- A quick **hip flick resets** the body back to fighting position
- The forward momentum of the weight transfer is captured and used to spring back

## Key Detail: Weight Past Midline

Unlike the jab and straight where weight transfers front-to-back, the hook's weight transfer moves **laterally past the body's midline**. This lateral momentum is what gives the hook its characteristic whipping power but also requires the braking and reset mechanism to maintain balance.
