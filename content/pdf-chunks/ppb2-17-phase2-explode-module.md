# PPB 2.0: Module 3 — Phase 2: Explode (Opening/Closing the Hips)

**Source:** Power Punching Blueprint 2.0 by Alex Wiant DC (rough draft, Pages 26-28)

Explode phase includes two things: **(1) the rebound after the dip, (2) hip rotation.**

**Opening of the hips powers your lead hand punches. Closing of the hips powers your rear hand punches.**

## Exercise 1: Opening and Closing the Hips

(Alex credits this exercise to **Peter Ralston, founder of Cheng Hsin**.) Turn all the way in both directions. Maintain a slight hinge throughout.

- **Closed position**: Rotate so your belt buckle points all the way forward, weight supported mostly by the lead leg, pivoting on the back foot. Both femurs internally rotated all the way. Body angled slightly forward, weight ~60:40 on the lead leg.
- **Open position**: From closed, push off your lead leg and rotate as far as you can to the side while pivoting on the back foot. Keep the lead foot planted, knee pointed in the direction of the toes. Belt buckle ends pointing ~90° to the side, weight fairly even (perhaps slightly rear). Both femurs externally rotated all the way.

Key detail: **using a flat lead leg for the open phase utilizes ground reaction force** and is necessary for a sequentially accelerating lead hook or uppercut. The closing phase with slight forward body lean is needed later for stable impact and weight transfer.

**Do this exercise 100 times per day in each stance — orthodox and southpaw.** After a few thousand repetitions it becomes natural.

## Exercise 2: Adding an Explosive Start to Hip Rotation

Add the dip-and-bounce to the open/close:

- Practice dipping on the back foot, bouncing off, then closing the hips all the way. Keep some aggressive body lean in the closed position, supported by the lead leg — the lead leg uses the ground to stabilize the body and arrest forward movement
- From closed, dip and bounce off the lead leg (foot on the ground, knee tracking the toes) and complete the opening movement
- **Focus on leading with your athletic center** (the spot about two inches below your belly button in the center of your pelvis) — this creates the hip/torso separation necessary for generating torque and turning that torque into acceleration
- Combine into one seamless fluid movement: dip/bounce off rear leg → close; dip/bounce off lead leg → open

When actually punching this movement may be more subtle — the exaggeration now is training you to **use your glutes to power your punches**. Sometimes you won't dip at all before a punch (e.g. linking the 1-2 or 2-3), but the dip is there when you want extra speed/power, want to wait a beat, or slip defensively before following up.

## Exercise 3: Feeling the Closed Position (Stability)

Some students rotate too far laterally over the lead leg and lose balance — **the closed position should feel very stable**. The fix is almost a one-legged "good morning":

- Hold a dumbbell/kettlebell, or put an empty or light barbell on your shoulders (do both variations for varied stimulus)
- Get into the fully closed position with some body lean over the lead leg
- Feel the low back and lead leg engage to support the weight — **especially the lead-leg glute**
- Bend the knee and hip a bit more to engage the muscles; get comfortable here
- **This position is where you will be after throwing your rear hand punches**
