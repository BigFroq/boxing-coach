# PPB 2.0: The Stretch Shortening Cycle

**Source:** Power Punching Blueprint 2.0 by Alex Wiant DC (rough draft, Page 6)

## The Mechanism

All human movement is created through series of stretches and contractions. When you do this quickly you can increase force production through **elastic muscle energy**.

You can see this phenomenon at work when:
- Someone bounces a barbell off their chest in a bench press
- The fast dip before a jump
- The quick stretch and rebound at the end of a golf backswing to initiate the downswing

The quick stretch loads the stretching muscle with elastic muscle energy which you can then release with a stronger, more violent contraction. **The quick stretch essentially loads your muscle like a spring so it can rebound with a faster, harder contraction.** This mechanism is at work in virtually every fast athletic movement.

## Important Clarification: "Stretch and Contract" Is Not a Conscious Contraction

When Alex says "stretch and contract" for the cross-body chains of the core (connecting pelvis and torso), this "contraction" is NOT a real conscious contraction like an ab crunch or side bend:

- When the hips rotate, the abs and obliques are stretched across the body
- This creates an **elastic rebound effect** that transfers and amplifies the hip rotation through the core and torso
- When you get the timing and feel, **it should feel like a throw**, rather than an intentional "contraction"

## At Impact: Reflexive Bracing

At impact these chains tighten **reflexively** to stabilize the body and support weight transfer. It should feel like "bracing against force" — the same feeling you get in your core when you push a car or lean into a wall with your fist. Your cross-body chains have to contract to support your body's weight in these positions.

You do NOT have to intentionally "contract" your cross-body muscles when punching.
