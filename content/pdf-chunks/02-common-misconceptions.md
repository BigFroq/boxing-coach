# Common Misconceptions Debunked

**Source:** Power Punching Blueprint by Alex Wiant DC (Pages 2-6)

## "Put Your Shoulder Into the Punch"

**Wrong approach:** In a straight line movement, your shoulder generates power (military press, bench press).

**Reality:** In a punch, the shoulder TRANSFERS energy, it doesn't generate it. You create torque with a hip rotation, then that gets your core rotating and as the energy transfers up your kinetic muscle chains to your arm and fist, it goes through the shoulder. Trying to "put your shoulder into the punch" encourages you to look at the punch as a straight line movement, which it is not.

## "Power Comes from the Shoulder Pop"

**Wrong approach:** Focusing on popping the shoulder to generate power.

**Reality:** The shoulder "pop" is strictly a result of the shift in momentum and transfer of energy during weight transfer. It's not what generates power. Focusing on popping your shoulder:
- Encourages poor punch mechanics
- Compromises shoulder stability if not in neutral position when landing
- Disconnects you from the many muscles connecting arm bone to shoulder blade and torso

**Correct focus:** Transfer your energy through your target. Land with a very stable shoulder, utilizing as many of the connecting muscles as possible. Your shoulder should move as little as possible within the joint throughout the punch.

## "Step When You Punch"

**Partial truth:** When you punch correctly with power, you transfer a lot of weight and momentum through your target. With a jab or straight right, if you don't step forward, you will fall.

**Reality:** The step isn't what you should focus on. Transferring your weight forward through your target should be the focus. The step is a consequence of the weight transfer, not the cause of power.

## "Breathe Out When You Begin Your Punch"

**Wrong approach:** Breathing out at the initiation of the punch.

**Reality:** This is inaccurate and will actually weaken your punch.
- If you breathe out when you initiate, you set yourself up for generating power at the beginning of the movement, rather than the middle to end.
- When you breathe out, you lose intra-abdominal pressure, which you need for your core to activate and transfer power.
- It really doesn't matter when you breathe, because when you look at the punch as a throw, the muscles involved will work properly at their maximum capacity no matter where you are in the breath cycle.

## "Power Comes from the Heel"

**Partial truth:** When you push off your rear toes like a sprinter in the explosive phase, you should feel some tension in your heel.

**Reality:** The kinetic chain starts at your toes, wraps around your heel, and travels up the back of your leg into your strong hip muscles. It's the same feeling you get when you explode off your toes into a sprint. The heel doesn't have much to do with the saying itself.
