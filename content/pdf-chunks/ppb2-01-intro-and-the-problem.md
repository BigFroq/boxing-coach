# PPB 2.0: Introduction and The Problem — Boxing Trails Other Sports in Biomechanics

**Source:** Power Punching Blueprint 2.0 / Manual of Modern Boxing Biomechanics by Alex Wiant DC (rough draft, Pages 1-3)

## The Premise

Most boxers never punch with their full potential — even pros leave a lot on the table. Every muscle performs a specific action; chains of muscles link together through fascia to create **kinetic chains** which turn individual muscular function into connected systems working in concert to create movement, especially athletic movement.

The manual's origin: over 10 years ago, head coach Sarge asked Alex to teach his amateurs-turning-pro how to punch harder. That became the original Power Punching Blueprint. This 2.0 manual is the synthesis and culmination of a decade of refinement and expansion.

## Chapter 1: The Problem

Boxing trails behind other sports when it comes to biomechanics. **Big time.** Most major sports have sports scientists and biomechanics experts — swing coaches, quarterback throwing coaches, pitching coaches. Boxing really doesn't.

- Most boxing coaches coach based on what they learned in the past — "it's really a big game of telephone"
- Tips are vague: "transferring your weight," "stepping when you punch" — no coherent framework from basic principles of movement to efficient application
- Because coaches can't teach optimal biomechanics, they assume power is innate: "born with power," "natural puncher," "you're born with two things, the punch you give and the punch you take"
- The golf analogy: imagine a golf coach saying "you can't drive the ball 300 yards? Sorry kid, you have to be born with it." Nobody is born with a perfect golf swing or a 95 mph fastball — it's learned and practiced

## The Promise

If you are even minimally athletic, you can learn to organize your body effectively and efficiently to maximize punching power. Alex himself dealt with poor shoulder integrity and low back issues and still learned to move better and punch harder. With boxing, you don't need the equivalent power of a 95 mph fastball — you just need to be able to throw.

The manual rebuilds your biomechanics from the ground up — just like rebuilding someone's pitch, golf swing or tennis serve — using your structure (spine and skeleton), muscles, fascia and kinetic chains based on their most efficient roles.

## What 2.0 Covers (Roadmap)

- **Ground reaction force** — rebuilt from scratch: true ground-to-glove speed and power without "power leak" or loss of kinetic sequencing
- **Universal athletic concepts** — hip hinge, core muscle balance
- **Kinetic Integrated Mechanics (KIMs) system** — stretch shortening cycle, kinetic chains, the four phases of the punch; combining these concepts is "the sauce" of the program
- **The kinetic tether** — the vital connection of muscle and fascia between hips and arm allowing you to land with the full force of your entire body via continuous tension
- **Detailed punch breakdowns** — key concepts, takeaways, drills and exercises per punch
- **Applications and training principles** — shadowboxing best practices, punching independent of breath timing, integrating into combos on heavy bag or double end bag
