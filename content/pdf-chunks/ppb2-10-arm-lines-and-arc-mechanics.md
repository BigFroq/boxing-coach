# PPB 2.0: The Arm Lines and Arc Mechanics

**Source:** Power Punching Blueprint 2.0 by Alex Wiant DC (rough draft, Pages 13-14)

The arm lines are kinetic chains that connect the torso to the fist through the shoulder, arm and forearm muscles. They help **shape the arc of the punch, accelerate the arm and stabilize the fist at impact**.

**Even straight punches travel on a subtle arc rather than in a perfectly straight line.** The arm lines guide this trajectory while maintaining tension and stability through shoulder, elbow, wrist and fist.

## Main Functions in Punching

- Accelerate the arm through the punch arc
- Shape the trajectory of the punch
- Connect force generated by the hips and amplified by the torso into the arm and fist
- Create stability and rigidity at impact
- Help transfer weight efficiently into the target

## Key Structures

- **Pec major and minor** — major role in arm acceleration; shape the arm's movement through the punch arc. Can move the arm forward, across the body, and contribute to internal arm rotation. Both stabilize the shoulder and arm at impact.
- **Biceps** — contribute heavily to acceleration during **hooks and uppercuts**. As the fist travels through an arc, the biceps help **reduce the radius of the arc, increasing fist acceleration through the impact zone**.
- **Triceps** — part of the posterior arm line; extend the elbow during straight punches, help with downward arm trajectory and stability at impact.
- **Wrist flexors** — major role in **impact stability**. These muscles contract when you grip your fist and at impact when landing with shearing force from the arc. They help turn the arm and fist into a **rigid structure** capable of delivering effective weight transfer.

## Arc Mechanics

All punches travel on some degree of arc, even straight punches. This arc allows the body to accelerate the fist efficiently while creating **shearing force and stability at impact**. The arm lines coordinate the arc so force generated from the body transfers cleanly into the target.
