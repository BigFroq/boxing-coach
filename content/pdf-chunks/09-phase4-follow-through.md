# Phase 4: Follow Through

**Source:** Power Punching Blueprint by Alex Wiant DC (Page 21)

## The Unwinding

Phase 4 is the **turning over and unwinding of the arm** after the fist grab at impact. This is not a separate deliberate action — it is the natural continuation of the spiral motion that began in the earlier phases.

## Purpose of Follow Through

- **Follows the arc naturally** — the arm continues along its spiral path past the point of contact
- **Absorbs residual energy** — the body safely dissipates the force that was not transferred into the target
- **Prevents elbow jamming** — without follow through, the arm would stop abruptly at full extension, risking hyperextension and joint damage

## Returning to Neutral

Use the **forward weight transfer as a "load" to spring back to neutral position**. The momentum that carried the body forward during the punch becomes the elastic energy that pulls you back into your stance. This is not a reset — it is a rebound that keeps you in rhythm and ready for the next action.

## Hook Follow Through

The hook has a specific follow through pattern:

- **Posterior shoulder muscles brake** the arm after impact — the muscles on the back of the shoulder decelerate the horizontal rotation
- A **hip flick resets** the body back to neutral — a quick reverse rotation of the hips snaps the body back to fighting position

This braking and resetting mechanism prevents over-rotation and keeps you balanced and ready for the next punch in a combination.
