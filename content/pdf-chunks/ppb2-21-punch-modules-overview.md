# PPB 2.0: Punch Modules Overview — Adaptability and Phase Overlap

**Source:** Power Punching Blueprint 2.0 by Alex Wiant DC (rough draft, Pages 35-36)

The punch descriptions are for the **basic mechanics** — the punches are highly adaptable once the basics are down. You'll be able to throw these punches from nearly any position. **The only thing you need is some weight on the GRF leg to create the initial hip rotation.**

- Your stance keeps you in a constantly "loaded" position
- The extra phase-1 load — a brief relaxation of the leg muscles to bend knee and hip on your GRF leg — adds speed and power on demand
- **It's vital to master the load phase; it needs to be fast and effortless.** It gives you the ability to fire punches from odd positions and to break rhythm to throw punches like the **false lead**

## Phase Overlap

There is overlap between phases 2 and 3: **the torso should begin rotating just after the hip motion starts, and the arm will often begin accelerating before the final position of the torso.** You're using your whole body — the punch closely resembles a throw or swing, not a sequence of discrete stops.
